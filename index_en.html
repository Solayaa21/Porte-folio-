Post-Production Dashboard: From Manual Tracking to Dynamic Management

Transformation of a task-tracking file into a usable database, with pivot tables and dashboards to analyze workload, productivity, and remaining time.

Manual Tracking
→
VBA Macro
→
Normalized DB
→

Management Dashboard

1. Context

The team tracked tasks in a detailed table, practical for daily monitoring but difficult to exploit for analysis. Information was scattered by stages, individuals, dates, and projects, making it complex to identify the most time-consuming tasks, demanding projects, and individual monthly performances.

2. Solution

I created a VBA macro capable of automatically transforming the tracking into a normalized database. This database then feeds pivot tables and a dashboard allowing analysis of volumes, times, projects, tasks, production stages, and performances by period, collaborator, or project.

Database generated via macro.
Analysis pivot tables.
Final productivity and workload analysis dashboard.

3. Result

Without adding any extra data entry for the team, the tool transforms existing data into actionable metrics. The team has a clear tool to understand where time was spent, anticipate remaining workload, compare performances, and better allocate tasks according to profiles and needs.

Automation of Custom Administrative PDF Documents

Creation of an Excel/VBA tool to automatically generate custom administrative documents from internal databases and pre-filled templates.

→

1. Context

Administrative teams often have to produce a high volume of custom documents containing recurring information. When these documents are filled manually, the process becomes time-consuming and increases the risk of errors, double entries, omissions, or inconsistencies between files.

2. Solution

I implemented a progressive feeding process for several structured databases (HR, projects, rates) to centralize the necessary information. The user selects rows in the database, clicks a button, and VBA macros automatically fill the templates and export them as PDFs.

Central database allowing selection of rows to export.
Final export of sorted and renamed PDFs in a target folder.
Document template pre-filled automatically with the correct data.

3. Result

The administrative process is centralized, faster, and more reliable. Teams reduce repetitive entries, limit errors, and can produce a high volume of custom documents in a few clicks.

Time tracking & revenue reporting

Time tracking, production monitoring, and revenue reporting

Contribution to an Excel/VBA tool linking employee tracked time to projects, billed tasks, production progress, and associated revenues.

Microsoft Forms
→
Time DB
→
Excel/VBA
→
Project Tracking
→
Revenues

1. Context

Management needed to accurately track time spent per project, per billable task, and per employee, then compare this data against sold hours. Without a structured system, it becomes hard to know if actual progress matches the budget, hiding operational and financial risks.

2. Solution

The system allows employees to log their time via Microsoft Forms. This data then feeds a centralized base, used in Excel/VBA to link declared time to projects, billable categories, hourly rates, sold hours, remaining hours, and generated revenues.

Quick entry form for teams (Microsoft Forms).
Automatic consolidation of times into an Excel database.
Dashboard cross-referencing spent time and associated revenues.

3. Result

The tool provides better visibility on actual project progress, consumption of sold hours, associated revenues, and potential variances. It allows the team to better manage production, identify at-risk projects, and continuously track profitability.
